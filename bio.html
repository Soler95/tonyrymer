Biography

The Artist

Tony Rymer is an internationally performing cellist known for expressive tone, refined interpretation and commanding stage presence across Europe and the United States. His playing bridges the classical tradition with a deeply personal voice, drawing audiences into a world where technique and emotion are inseparable.

A graduate of the Royal Academy of Music, Rymer studied under some of the most distinguished cellists of his generation. His formative years were defined by an insatiable curiosity — not only for the cello repertoire, but for the full spectrum of musical expression, from the architecture of Bach's suites to the raw expressionism of twentieth-century works.

"A cellist of rare emotional intelligence and striking sonic depth." — International Music Review
Early Life & Training

Born into a musical family, Rymer began his cello studies at the age of six. By sixteen he had won his first national competition, earning a full scholarship to the Junior Conservatoire. His early studies were marked by the influence of the great Romantic tradition, yet his teachers also encouraged him to engage deeply with the modernist canon — an approach that would define his later artistry.

His time at the Royal Academy of Music proved transformative. Under the guidance of internationally acclaimed faculty, Rymer developed the technical mastery and interpretive depth that now characterise his performances. He graduated with distinction, winning the Academy's highest prize for solo performance.

International Career

Rymer's professional career has taken him to the world's most prestigious venues — from the Berlin Philharmonie and Carnegie Hall to the Royal Albert Hall and the Concertgebouw in Amsterdam. He has appeared as soloist with leading orchestras across Europe and the United States, performing concertos from Dvořák and Elgar to Shostakovich and Lutosławski.

As a chamber musician, Rymer is equally celebrated. He is a founding member of the Auryn Piano Trio, with whom he has recorded three critically acclaimed albums and toured extensively across three continents. His collaborations with fellow soloists are described by critics as marked by a rare musical generosity and depth of communication.

Beyond the concert platform, Rymer is a passionate advocate for contemporary music. He has premiered works written for him by leading composers of his generation, and maintains close working relationships with living composers — believing that the future of classical music lies in the dialogue between past and present.
